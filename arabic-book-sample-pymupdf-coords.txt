Section 1.1
النَحَوَ – Arabic Grammar
Definition:
Nahw is that science, which teaches us how to join a noun, verb and particle to form a correct
sentence, as well as what theإِاعرَاب (condition) of the last letter of a word should be.
Subject Matter:
Its subject matter is
كَلِمَة(word) and
كََلَم(sentence).
Objective:
 The immediate objective is to learn how to read, write and speak Arabic correctly, and
to avoid making mistakes in this.  For example,مَياد,دَار,َدَخَل, andِف are four words.
The science of Nahw teaches us how to put them together to form a correct sentence.
 The mid-term objective is to use our Arabic skills to understand the Qur’an, Hadeeth,
Fiqh and other Islamic sciences, so that we can act upon them.
 The ultimate objective through the above is to gain the pleasure of Allah Most High.

الَكَلَمَةَ – The Word
Any word uttered by humans is called aلَافظ. If it has a meaning, it is called
مَاوضُاوع
(meaningful); and if it does not have any meaning, it is calledمُاهمَل (meaningless).
In Arabic,
لَافظ
مَاوضُاوع (meaningful word) is of two types:مُافرَد andمُرََكّب.
1.مُافرَد – Singular: It is that single word, which conveys one meaning.  It is also called
كَلِمَة.
2.مُرََكّب – Compound: It is a group of words.  They may form a complete sentence or an
incomplete one.
Types ofَكلمة
There are three types ofكَلِمَة:
1.
ااسم (noun)
2.ِفاعل (verb)
3.
حارف (particle)
اسم – Noun:
 It is thatكَلِمَة whose meaning can be understood without the need to combine it with
another word.
 It does not have any tense.1
 It is the name of a person, place or thing.2
e.g.رَجُل
man
اَلابَايت
house
Note: A noun can never have aتَانوِيان (tanween) and anال at the same time.

 It is thatكَلِمَة whose meaning can be understood without the need to combine it with
another word.
 It has one of the three tenses: past, present, or future.3
 It denotes an action.4
e.g.

Note: Aفِاعل can never have aتَانوِيان (tanween) or anال.

Section 1.3
أقساماَْلَسَم – Types of Noun
اِاسم is of three types:
1.
جامِد – Primary Noun: It is thatاِاسم which is neither derived from another word nor is any
word derived from it.
e.g.
فَرَس
horse
بِانت
girl
2.
ماصدَر – Root Noun: It is thatاِاسم from which many words are derived.
e.g.
ضَارب
to hit
نَاصر
to help
3.
ماشتَق – Derived Noun: It is thatاِاسم which is derived from a masdar.
e.g.
ضَارِب hitter
مَانصُاور
the helped
أقسامَالَفَعَل – Types of Verb
فِاعل is of four types:
1.
اَلامَاضاي
Past tense
e.g.
ضَرَب
He hit.
2.
اَلامُضَارِعُ
Present and Future tense
e.g.
يَاضرِب
He is hitting or will
hit.
3.
اَالامرُ
Positive Command/
Imperative
e.g.
اِاضرِاب
Hit!
4.اَلَنّاهيُ
Negative Command/
Prohibitive
e.g.
لَْتَاضراب
Don’t hit!
أقساماَلَرَف – Types of Particle
حَارف is of two types:
1.
عامِل – Causative Particle: It is thatحَارف, which causesإِاعرَاب change in the word after it.
e.g.
مَيادفِالامَاسجِد
Zayd is in the mosque.
2.
غايرُالاعَامِلِ – Non-Causative: It is thatحَارف, which does not causeإِاعرَاب change in the word
after it.
e.g.
ث
then
و
and
