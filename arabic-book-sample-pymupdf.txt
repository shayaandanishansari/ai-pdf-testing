CHAPTER 1 
 
 
 
Section 1.1 
َ اَ لنَ حَ و – Arabic Grammar 
Definition: 
Nahw is that science, which teaches us how to join a noun, verb and particle to form a correct 
sentence, as well as what the   ابَعارِإ (condition) of the last letter of a word should be. 
 
Subject Matter:  
Its subject matter is 
 
  ةَمِلَك(word) and 
 
  مَلََك(sentence). 
 
 
Objective:  
 The immediate objective is to learn how to read, write and speak Arabic correctly, and 
to avoid making mistakes in this.  For example, يادَم, ارَد,  َلَخَد, and  ِف are four words.  
The science of Nahw teaches us how to put them together to form a correct sentence. 
 The mid-term objective is to use our Arabic skills to understand the Qur’an, Hadeeth, 
Fiqh and other Islamic sciences, so that we can act upon them.   
 The ultimate objective through the above is to gain the pleasure of Allah Most High.

Section 1.2 
َ اَ لَ كَ لَ مَ ة – The Word 
Any word uttered by humans is called a  فاظَل. If it has a meaning, it is called 
 واَم  واعُض 
(meaningful); and if it does not have any meaning, it is called   لَهامُم (meaningless).  
In Arabic, 
 فاظَل 
  واعُواضَم (meaningful word) is of two types:   دَفارُم and   بَّكَرُم. 
1.   دَفارُم – Singular: It is that single word, which conveys one meaning.  It is also called 
  ةَمِلَك. 
2.   بَّكَرُم – Compound: It is a group of words.  They may form a complete sentence or an 
incomplete one.     
 
Types of  َ ة م ل ك 
There are three types of   ةَمِلَك:  
1. 
 ِا اس  م (noun)  
2.  ِف اع  ل (verb)  
3. 
 َح ار  ف (particle) 
 َ م س ا – Noun: 
 It is that   ةَمِلَك whose meaning can be understood without the need to combine it with 
another word. 
 It does not have any tense.1 
 It is the name of a person, place or thing.2 
e.g.   لُجَر 
man 
 
 
 
 ُياتَ لابَا 
house 
Note: A noun can never have a   يانِو ناَ ت (tanween) and an ال at the same time.
